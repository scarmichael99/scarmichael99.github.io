Linear Motion Infinite Practice Worksheet


Instructions

Solve the following problems; if you need more practice, refresh your browser (or close and reopen this page) for all new numbers. Treat all givens as though they have two sig figs.

Equations

   

\[ \begin{aligned} \overline{speed} & = \frac{distance}{t} \end{aligned} \]
\[ \begin{aligned} \overline{v} & = \frac{\Delta x}{t} \end{aligned} \]
\[ \begin{aligned} a & = \frac{(v_f - v_i)}{t} \end{aligned} \]
\[ \begin{aligned} v_f & = v_i + at \end{aligned} \]
\[ \begin{aligned} \Delta x & = \overline{v}\times t = \frac{(v_i + v_f)}{2} \times t \end{aligned} \]
\[ \begin{aligned} \Delta x &= v_i t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2 \end{aligned} \]
\[ \begin{aligned} \Delta x &= v_f t - \frac{1}{2} a t^2 \end{aligned} \]
\[ \begin{aligned} {v_f} ^2 &= {v_i} ^2 + 2a \Delta x \end{aligned} \]

Problems

1) Constant Velocity Motion I

How far does an object travel if it maintains a constant speed of m/s for seconds?

First, list your givens:
(We'll call the direction of motion the positive direction.)

$v =$ + m/s
$t =$ s
$a = 0$
$\Delta x =$ ?

We will use the equation \[ \begin{aligned} \Delta x &= v_i t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2 \end{aligned} \]
Since the velocity is constant, the acceleration is zero; so we're left with the equation \[ \begin{aligned} \Delta x &= v_i \times t \end{aligned} \] and plug in everything that is given, finding that,

$\Delta x$ = m/s $\times$ s = m = m


2) Constant Velocity Motion II

How much time would it take you to travel m if you were traveling at a constant rate of m/s?


First, list your givens:
(We'll call the direction of motion the positive direction.)

$\Delta x =$ + m
$v =$ + m/s
$a = 0$
$t =$ ?

We will use the equation \[ \begin{aligned} \Delta x &= v_i t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2 \end{aligned} \]
Since the velocity is constant, the acceleration is zero; so we're left with the equation \[ \begin{aligned} \Delta x &= v_i \times t \end{aligned} \] and plug in everything that is given, finding that,

m = m/s $\times$ $t$


To solve for time we must divide both sides by m/s, which gives us

$t$ = m $\div$ m/s = s = s

3) Accelerated Motion I

A rocket going m/s speeds up at a rate of m/s2 for seconds.

a) How fast is the rocket then moving?
b) How far did the rocket travel while it accelerated?



First, list your givens:
(We'll call the direction of motion the positive direction.)

$v_i =$ + m/s
$a =$ + m/s2
(We know the acceleration is in the same direction as the velocity because the rocket is speeding up;
if the rocket had been slowing down, that would mean the acceleration was in the opposite (i.e. negative) direction.)
$t =$ s

To solve part (a) we will use the equation \[ \begin{aligned} v_f = v_i + at \end{aligned} \]
Plugging in we get

$v_f$ = m/s + m/s2 $\times$ s = m/s = m/s


To solve for (b) we will use the equation

$\Delta x = v_i t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2$


Plugging in we get

$\Delta x$ = m/s $\times$ s + .5 $\times$ m/s2 $\times$ ( s)2
$\Delta x$ = m = m



4) Accelerated Motion II

A car driving west at a speed of m/s accelerates in the eastward direction at a rate of m/s2 for seconds.

a) How fast and in what direction is the car now moving?
b) How far did the car travel while it accelerated?



Since we have two different directions in this problem, we will need to call one direction positive and one direction negative. For this problem, I will call west the positive direction and east the negative direction, but you could just as easily do the opposite.

Next, list your givens:

$v_i$ = + m/s
$a$ = m/s2
$t$ = s

To solve part (a) we will use the equation \[ \begin{aligned} v_f = v_i + at \end{aligned} \]
Plugging in we get:

$v_f$ = + m/s + m/s2 $\times$ s = + m/s = m/s



To solve for (b) we will use the equation

$\Delta x = v_i t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2$


Plugging in we get

$\Delta x$ = ( m/s $\times$ s) + (.5 $\times$ m/s2 $\times$ ( s)2)
$\Delta x$ = m = m





5) Free Fall I

You drop a pebble from rest off a cliff and find that it takes the pebble seconds to hit the ground. You know that everything in free fall accelerates at a rate of 9.8 m/s2 down. Ignoring air resistance,

a) How fast is the pebble moving when it strikes the ground? and
b) How tall is the cliff?



First, list your givens:
(I'm going to make down positive and up negative for this problem.)

$v_i =$ 0 m/s (because you dropped the pebble)
$a =$ +9.8 m/s2
$t =$ s

To solve part (a) we will use the equation \[ \begin{aligned} v_f = v_i + at \end{aligned} \]
Plugging in we get

$v_f$ = 0 m/s + 9.8 m/s2 $\times$ s = + m/s = m/s (down)


To solve for (b) we will use the equation

$\Delta x = v_i t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2$


Plugging in we get

$\Delta x$ = 0 m/s $\times$ s + .5 $\times$ 9.8 m/s2 $\times$ ( s)2
$\Delta x$ = + m = m



6) Free Fall II

You throw a ball downward off a building with an initial speed of m/s and it accelerates downward at a rate of 9.8 m/s2. It takes the ball s to hit the ground. Ignoring air resistance,

a) How fast is the ball moving when it strikes the ground? and
b) How tall is the building?



First, list your givens:
(Since everything's down in this problem, I'm going to make down the positive direction and up the negative direction.)

$v_i =$ + m/s
$a =$ +9.8 m/s2
$t =$ s



To solve part (a) we will use the equation \[ \begin{aligned} v_f = v_i + at \end{aligned} \]
Plugging in we get

$v_f$ = m/s + 9.8 m/s2 $\times$ s = m/s = m/s


To solve for (b) we will use the equation

$\Delta x = v_i t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2$


Plugging in we get

$\Delta x$ = m/s $\times$ s + .5 $\times$ 9.8 m/s2 $\times$ ( s)2
$\Delta x$ = m = m



8) Distance vs. Displacement

You travel m west in s, stop for s, then travel m east in s. Find:

(a) Your distance traveled and
(b) your displacement



(a) Let's start by finding your distance traveled, which is simply the sum of all the individual distances in the problem.

$distance =$ m $+$ m $=$ m

(b) Displacement is a little trickier since it's a vector. Since you walked in two opposite directions, we'll subtract the distances and use the direction of the bigger vector.

$displacement =$ $\lvert$ m $-$ m $\rvert =$ m
